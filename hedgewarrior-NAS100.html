Cycle Outcomes:
Each “cycle” is one full sequence from initial entry to complete exit (all positions closed and the EA ready to start a new set). Most cycles finish with no hedge, but if price moves against you, the EA may hedge one or more times before finally closing the cycle.

What is Hedging?
Hedging in this EA means that if the price moves against your initial position by a set amount, the EA opens a trade in the opposite direction. Instead of closing your losing position for a guaranteed loss, the EA manages both sides, aiming to either reach breakeven or a small gain if the price oscillates (moves back and forth) enough. This method avoids locking in a loss immediately, but it is not a guarantee of loss avoidance—if price continues to oscillate without a clear trend or direction, or fails to revert, the EA may be unable to exit without a loss. Each new hedge increases total exposure and can lead to significant drawdown if the market ranges or oscillates for a long period or moves unfavorably for extended periods. The goal is to manage adverse moves and seek an exit without a loss, but this comes at the risk of large drawdowns when conditions are unfavorable.
